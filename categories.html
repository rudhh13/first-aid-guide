Essential First Aid & Safety Tips

Be prepared to respond to medical emergencies with confidence.

What is First Aid?

First aid is the **immediate assistance** given to someone suffering from an injury or illness before professional medical help arrives. It can be the difference between life and death in emergencies.

Basic Principles of First Aid:

  • **Preserve Life** – Ensure the injured person is breathing and out of immediate danger.
  • **Prevent Condition from Worsening** – Take necessary measures to stop bleeding, immobilize fractures, etc.
  • **Promote Recovery** – Provide comfort and reassurance until medical help arrives.

Common First Aid Situations & Tips

Burns

Steps:

  1. Cool the burn under running **cold water for at least 10 minutes**.
  2. Remove **tight clothing or jewelry** near the affected area.
  3. Cover with a **non-stick bandage** or sterile dressing.
  4. Do **not** apply ice, butter, or ointments.
  5. Seek medical help for **severe burns**.

Cuts & Wounds

Steps:

  1. Apply **direct pressure** to stop bleeding.
  2. Wash the wound with **clean water**.
  3. Apply an **antiseptic and cover** with a bandage.
  4. Seek medical help if **bleeding doesn’t stop** or if the wound is deep.

Fractures

Steps:

  1. Keep the injured person still and **immobilize the affected area**.
  2. Apply a **splint or support** to stabilize the bone.
  3. Avoid moving the injured limb excessively.
  4. Seek immediate **medical help**.

Choking

Steps:

  1. Encourage the person to **cough**.
  2. If coughing doesn’t work, perform the **Heimlich maneuver**.
  3. For infants, use **back blows and chest thrusts**.
  4. Call emergency services if the person **cannot breathe**.

Heart Attack

Steps:

  1. Call **emergency services immediately**.
  2. Make the person **sit down and stay calm**.
  3. If conscious, give them **aspirin** to chew (if not allergic).
  4. Perform **CPR** if the person is unresponsive and not breathing.
